# Supply Chain Optimization Case Study

## Overview
This case study was developed as part of an interview assessment using a fictional company (“Tool Depot”) and simulated data.

The analysis evaluates supplier selection and shipping strategy for a new product, focusing on optimizing total cost while ensuring scalability as demand grows.

---

## Problem Context

Tool Depot needed to select a supplier for a new product (hammers) to be introduced across multiple locations.

Key constraints included:
- Two suppliers with different unit costs  
- Two shipping carriers with different pricing structures (flat-rate vs. weight-based)  
- Fixed shipment capacity limits  
- Projected 10% annual demand growth  
- A requirement to select a single supplier for the upcoming year  

---

## Objective

- Identify the most cost-effective supplier and shipping strategy  
- Optimize total landed cost (product + transportation)  
- Evaluate how cost behavior changes under demand growth  
- Provide a recommendation that remains effective over time, not just at current volume  

---

## Approach

- Compared supplier pricing and carrier cost structures  
- Evaluated total landed cost across all supplier-carrier combinations  
- Identified unused capacity within existing shipping loads  
- Modeled growth scenarios to assess long-term impact on cost and capacity  
- Analyzed trade-offs between unit cost and transportation efficiency  

---

## Key Insights

- Cost optimization is driven more by shipping efficiency than unit price alone  
- Supplier B’s slightly higher unit cost is offset by improved logistics efficiency  
- Leveraging Carrier X’s flat-rate capacity reduces marginal shipping cost  
- As demand increases, variable shipping costs scale faster than flat-rate capacity  
- A cost inflection point emerges where initially cheaper options become less effective over time  

---

## Recommendation

**Primary Recommendation:**
- Select Supplier B and utilize Carrier X to optimize total landed cost through improved logistics efficiency  

**Strategic Recommendation:**
- Implement a pilot split-shipment program to validate how cost and capacity behavior change as volume increases  
- Maintain optionality between suppliers and carriers to reduce long-term risk  

---

## Strategy Rationale

While some options appear cheaper in the short term, the analysis shows that cost performance changes as demand scales.

As shipment volume increases, variable shipping costs can grow faster than flat-rate capacity, creating a cost inflection point where initial savings diminish.

This introduces a risk that selecting a strategy based solely on initial cost could lead to higher total cost over the life of a long-term contract.

The pilot program allows this behavior to be tested using real-world data, ensuring that the selected strategy remains financially and operationally effective as the system scales.

---

## Business Impact

- Reduced overall supply chain cost through system-level optimization  
- Improved utilization of existing shipping capacity  
- Proactively identified long-term cost risks before they materialize  
- Enabled more resilient decision-making under growth and changing conditions  
- Shifted decision-making from short-term cost to long-term efficiency
